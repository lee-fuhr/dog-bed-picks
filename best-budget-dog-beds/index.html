A good dog bed doesn’t have to cost a pile of biscuits. Budget-friendly models can still provide solid support, washable covers, and years of use, you just need to know what to prioritise. For most families, the key factors are size (measure your dog’s length and sprawl), filling type (memory foam or polyfill), and how easy the cover is to remove and clean. Keep in mind that a bed that seems cheap upfront may cost more in the long run if it flattens quickly or can’t be laundered. The three picks here all have strong owner reviews and a reputation for holding up better than their price tags suggest. Choose based on your dog’s sleeping style, chewing habits, and any joint needs.

Best for specific needs

Best for anxious or burrowing dogs

If your dog loves to nestle into a donut-shaped cushion or tuck their nose under the rim, the Best Friends by Sheri Orthopedic Calming Dog Bed is built exactly for that. The raised edges create a sense of security that many dogs find calming, and the interior is soft enough to snuggle into without losing shape. Owners of rescues and nervous dogs consistently report it helps settle their pets faster at bedtime. Our pick: Best Friends by Sheri Orthopedic Calming Dog Bed.

Best for durability on a budget

For dogs that aren’t gentle on their beds, moderates scratchers or those who drag it around the room, the K&H Original Pet Bed offers a tougher-than-average cover at a very low price. Its simple cushion design means fewer seams and zippers to fail, and the cover survives repeat machine washes without pilling. It won’t win beauty contests, but it will probably outlast the dog’s current phase. Our pick: K&H Pet Products Original Pet Bed.

Best for joint support on a budget

The Casper Dog Bed uses layered foam that mimics the company’s human mattress formula, giving it a rare combination of support and pressure relief at its price point. Older dogs or those with arthritis often sleep more soundly on it compared to basic polyfill beds, and the cooling cover helps prevent overheating. If you can stretch your budget slightly, this bed rewards you with longer-lasting structural integrity. Our pick: Casper Dog Bed.

Our verdict

For most dogs, the Best Friends by Sheri Orthopedic Calming Dog Bed delivers the best bang for your buck with its dual-action calming and orthopedic design. If your main concerns are simplicity and toughness, the K&H Original Pet Bed gets the job done without fuss. And if you have a few extra dollars to spend on upgraded joint support, the Casper Dog Bed is a genuine step up that still respects a budget. Whichever you choose, measuring your dog and their sleeping style first will make sure the bed gets used, not ignored.
